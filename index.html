Announcing the world debut of a never-before-experienced immersive storytelling event – a theatrical reimagining of award-winning author Philip Pullman’s Grimm Tales for Young and Old in Shoreditch Town Hall, London.

We invite you to experience a handful of classic fairytales in the flesh. From Little Red Riding Hood to Rapunzel, you’ll brush shoulders with unforgettable characters from Philip Pullman’s book, in a theatre production that will enchant and inspire children and adults of all ages. This is an Easter experience full of unexpected twists and turns, well suited to adults with a keen sense of curiosity and their brave children.

Infused with storytelling magic, immersive action and raw excitement, this is a way to see these classic fairytales like never before.

About The Show

A word from the author:

Philip Pullman (author of Grimm Tales for Young and Old):

“Nowadays we think of these wonderful tales as something from a book, but the great collection of the Brothers Grimm was published only two hundred years ago. The tales are much older than that, and they were originally told orally, not written down, so a dramatic production seems like a return to their origin. I’m sure the tales will flourish in this setting, and I greatly look forward to seeing how they come to life.”

What to expect, from our Creative Production Team:

Director Philip Wilson (Salisbury Playhouse):

“This immersive production will translate the exuberant pace of the stories’ plots into raw action, just as vividly as Pullman and the Grimm Brothers did in turning spoken fairy tales into the prevailing page-turners of their time.”

Producer Val Coward (Old Vic Tunnels):

“The basement rooms of the iconic Shoreditch Town Hall are a blank canvas backdrop for Pullman’s Grimm Tales. It’s a real flesh-and-blood rendering for story-hungry audiences of all ages; you’re literally in the tower with Rapunzel and the witch, or deep in the forest, sharing the magic with the players as each tale unfolds.”

The Tales

Little Red Riding Hood

Everyone’s favourite ‘don’t talk to strangers’ morality tale. Wantonly wandering off the path, our red-hooded heroine meets a wolf – the repercussions of which include some unlikely cross-dressing and some lovably naïve observations from Little Red. Thank heavens for the strapping huntsman.

The Three Snake Leaves

A warning against misguided romantic love, this tale sees a soldier so blindly devoted to his ungrateful princess wife that he cannot see her wickedness. When a snake shows him the neat party trick of reviving her with three leaves, the results show the soldier all’s not fair in love at all.

Rapunzel

Rapunzel is our lengthy-locked damsel, lost by a foolish father to a wayward witch. While doing time in the tower, she’s wooed by a prince who figures out the tricky rhyming code to climbing up to the turret. Our lovers are thwarted by the nasty old witch but, as you know by now, love will always prevail over evil. Fact.

The Juniper Tree

An unassuming tree is the silent witness to a familial drama – the likes of which you’re unlikely to expect. When his devoted mother dies, a boy’s new step-mum proves to be a mean matriarch indeed, with a number of extremely anti-social tendencies. When things get hairy the boy turns into a bird and tweets all her secrets across town.

Hans-My-Hedgehog

This tale about the fallibility of humans sees half-man, half-hedgehog Hans suffering greatly from barbed rejections and spiky prejudice. He leaves his uncaring family behind for the outdoors life, happy to serenade pigs and donkeys on the bagpipes. So when a couple of kings (with pretty princess daughters) ask him the way, they’re skewered by Hans’ wily test of friendship.

Cast and Creative

Philip Wilson
Adaptor & Director

Recent credits include Toro! Toro! (national tour), The Three Lions (Pleasance, Edinburgh), Twist of Gold (Polka), 66 Books (Bush), Earnest / Travesties (Birmingham Rep) and The Norman Conquests (Liverpool Playhouse). Philip was the Artistic Director of Salisbury Playhouse, 2007-11.

Valerie Coward
Producer

Producer for the Old Vic Tunnels, 20 years of experience in producing immersive theatre shows ranging from transforming Leadenhall Market into a Ballroom, to creating a circus out of a City Atrium.

Catherine Botibol
Executive Producer

Shortlisted in 2011 for the Hospital 100 list of emerging influential people in the creative and media industries, Cat is Creative Development Director of pd3 - a creative agency with 15 years experience creating entertainment content and experiences for brands such as O2, Sony, AEG, Live Nation, Ray-ban, Nike and Victorinox.

Tom Rogers
Set & Costume Designer

Tom trained at Motley. His most recent production include; Happy Days - A New Musical (National Tour), My Judy Garland Life (Nottingham Playhouse), Moominland Midwinter (Theatre Royal Bath), The Recruiting Officer (Salisbury Playhouse) and Witches Of Eastwick (Watermill).

Richard Hammarton
Sound Designer

Recent work includes Sizwe Bansi is Dead (Young Vic) and Fault Lines (Hampstead Theatre). Richard’s music has been heard on the BBC and ITV in Britain and WGBH in America.

Howard Hudson
Lighting Director

Howard received the Off West End Award for Best Lighting Design in 2011 and 2012. Recent work includes La Cage Aux Folles (Aarhus, Denmark); Cornelius (59E59, New York); Toro! Toro! (UK Tour); Long Day's Journey into Night (City Hall, Bermuda); Titanic (Southwark Playhouse).

Transport Links

Underground

The nearest underground station to Shoreditch Town Hall is Old Street, on the Northern Line. Old Street station is a simple five-minute walk along Old Street from the Town Hall. When you arrive at Old Street, exit through Exit 2 and head directly down Old Street towards the white tower, above the Town Hall. You can also get to the Town Hall from London Liverpool Street and Moorgate, which are each approximately 15 - 20 minutes’ walk away.

Overground

The nearest train station to the Town Hall is Shoreditch High Street or Hoxton. Each is approx 5-10 minutes’ walk away.

Buses

A number of London Buses go via Old Street, which makes the Town Hall extremely accessible via bus. Buses stopping nearby include numbers: 26, 35, 47, 48, 55, 67, 78, 135, 149, 242 and 243.

Parking

Shoreditch Town Hall does not have designated parking areas for clients or visitors to the Town Hall. There are a number of car parks nearby, including an NCP on Great Eastern Street. There is a short-term loading bay for clients of the Town Hall available outside the front of the venue, available between 10am-4pm daily, for a maximum of 15 minutes.
